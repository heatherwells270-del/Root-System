*This document was written to last. It uses legal definitions where precision matters,
plain language where clarity matters, and human language throughout —
because there is a human being on the other end of every interaction in this space.*

## 1. What This Platform Is

Root System is a free, open-source mutual aid tool. It is a digital community commons — a shared space for neighbors to exchange skills, goods, time, care, knowledge, and support.

It was created in good faith, given away freely under an open license, and is governed by the communities that choose to use and steward each deployment.

**Root System is not:**
- A commercial marketplace
- A dating or social platform
- An employment service
- A financial services platform
- A medical, legal, or professional services platform
- Affiliated with any political party, political movement, ideology, or corporation

**Root System is:**
- A tool for mutual aid between neighbors
- A time bank (one hour equals one hour, regardless of skill or background)
- A knowledge commons
- A free resource directory
- A space built to last

## 2. The Foundation: A Human Being Is Here

This platform was designed with a single non-negotiable premise:

**There is a human being on the other end of every interaction.**

Not a user profile. Not a handle. Not data. A person — with a history, a family, fears, needs, dignity, and a full interior life that no post, exchange, or flag will ever fully represent.

Every decision made in designing this platform, every term in this agreement, and every value stated here flows from that premise. When you flag a post, a person posted it. When you arrange an exchange, a person is bringing what they have to offer. When you read a need, a person wrote it.

Hold that. Always.

The creator bears no liability for content posted by users of any deployment and is not in a position to moderate, remove, or respond to content on platforms operated by others.

This legal protection exists to enable open community spaces. It does not diminish the moral responsibility of community members to uphold the values of this space and report genuine safety threats.

## 4. Prohibited Conduct

### 4.1 Hate Speech

This platform prohibits speech that targets individuals or groups based on:

- Race or color
- Ethnicity or national origin
- Religion or religious practice
- Sex or gender
- Gender identity or expression
- Sexual orientation
- Disability, whether physical or mental
- Immigration or citizenship status

Prohibited speech includes content that:

**(a) Dehumanizes** individuals or groups on the basis of the characteristics listed above — including but not limited to content that portrays such individuals or groups as subhuman, as inherently criminal, as inherently inferior, or as deserving of violence or exclusion.

**(b) Threatens or incites violence** against individuals or groups on the basis of the characteristics listed above. This standard is drawn from the true threats doctrine established in *Virginia v. Black*, 538 U.S. 343 (2003), which holds that true threats — statements where the speaker means to communicate a serious expression of intent to commit unlawful violence — are not protected expression.

**(c) Constitutes fighting words** as defined in *Chaplinsky v. New Hampshire*, 315 U.S. 568 (1942) — words that by their very utterance inflict injury or tend to incite an immediate breach of the peace, directed at an individual on the basis of the characteristics listed above.

**(d) Incites imminent lawless action** against individuals or groups on the basis of the characteristics listed above, applying the standard established in *Brandenburg v. Ohio*, 395 U.S. 444 (1969).

**(e) Constitutes harassment** — a pattern of conduct directed at a specific individual that is severe or pervasive enough to create a hostile environment, as defined under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and related federal law.

This platform also incorporates the protected class framework of the Matthew Shepard and James Byrd Jr. Hate Crimes Prevention Act (18 U.S.C. § 249), which establishes federal civil rights protections based on actual or perceived race, color, religion, national origin, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, and disability.

**What this does not prohibit:** Disagreement. Debate. Criticism of ideas, institutions, policies, or systems. Testimony about personal experience, including painful experience. Hard conversations about difficult subjects. Grief. Anger that doesn't target people. This platform was built for honest community, not sanitized community.

**Enforcement:** Posts meeting the hate speech standard will be removed. The posting handle will be flagged. Repeated violations will result in permanent removal from the network. Community members are encouraged to flag content that appears to meet this standard.

### 4.3 Suppression of Legitimate Voices

**The First Amendment to the United States Constitution** restricts government suppression of speech. It does not require private platforms to host all speech. However, this platform makes a values commitment that goes beyond legal minimum:

This platform will not be used as a tool to suppress, silence, dox, report, harass, or retaliate against legitimate voices — particularly voices that have been historically and systematically suppressed.

**Specifically prohibited:**
- Coordinating the targeting or harassment of any community member for speech they have a right to make
- Doxxing — publishing private identifying information about a community member without their consent
- Using the flagging system to silence legitimate posts rather than to report genuine violations
- Retaliating against community members who have flagged posts, reported safety incidents, or raised concerns
- Using this platform to organize campaigns of coordinated harassment against individuals or groups

**Specifically protected and centered:**

The voices of Black, Indigenous, and People of Color; LGBTQIA+ people; people with disabilities; immigrants and undocumented community members; people experiencing poverty; and all others whose voices have been systematically marginalized and suppressed take space in this community as a matter of explicit policy.

This is not a neutral statement and we do not pretend it is. Marginalized communities have been denied the benefits of mutual aid networks and community commons through deliberate exclusion. This platform was built with the explicit intent to reverse that. Not to exclude others — to ensure no one is excluded.

## 5. Core Values

### 5.1 Transparency and Honesty

The operating values of this space are openness, transparency, and honesty. Post what you actually have. Ask for what you actually need. Say what you mean. Correct yourself when you're wrong. Acknowledge when you don't know something.

This applies to the platform itself. The code is open source. The terms are public. The safety architecture is documented. There are no hidden systems, hidden data collection, or hidden incentives. What you see is what exists.

### 5.2 High Integrity

We hold ourselves to high integrity in every interaction. Integrity here means:

- Honoring the agreements you make through this network
- Following through on offers you post
- Being honest about your capacity and your needs
- Not using the community's trust to advance private interests
- Acknowledging harm if you cause it and making repair where possible

### 5.3 Empathy and Accountability Together

Empathy without accountability is empty. Accountability without empathy is punitive. This community practices both.

When harm occurs in this community — and it will, because humans are in it — the response is: name it clearly, address it directly, support the person harmed, and where possible, help the person who caused harm to understand what happened. Permanent removal exists for repeated or severe violations. It is not the first response to every failure.

### 5.4 Built to Last

This platform was designed with the assumption that it will outlive its creator, its original deployment, and many of the specific conditions of the moment it was built in.

That means: no trend language, no references to current political moments, no dependency on specific platforms or corporations. The values stated here are durable because they are grounded in human dignity, not in any particular cultural moment.

Communities that fork and deploy this tool inherit its values. They may adapt them for their community. They may not remove them.

## 6. Centering Marginalized Voices — A Specific Commitment

This section exists because general statements about inclusion often function to avoid specific commitments. This platform makes specific commitments:

**Black communities** have built mutual aid infrastructure in America since before its founding — often as a survival necessity in the face of exclusion from public systems. This platform recognizes and honors that history. Black voices and Black community needs are centered here, not as a special category but as a foundational one.

**Indigenous communities** have practiced gift economy, communal land stewardship, and mutual obligation across generations and in the face of sustained genocidal pressure. The values this platform is built on are not new — they are ancient, and their most durable forms are Indigenous forms. Indigenous sovereignty, Indigenous knowledge, and Indigenous ways of organizing community are respected and protected here.

**LGBTQIA+ communities** have built extraordinary mutual aid infrastructure historically — caring for each other during health crises, job discrimination, family rejection, and state violence when no other infrastructure existed. LGBTQIA+ voices and needs are explicitly protected and centered here.

**People with disabilities** are not a special interest group — they are community members with expertise, knowledge, and contributions that abled communities routinely fail to access. Accessibility is a design value of this platform, not an afterthought. Disability justice perspectives shape how we think about care, interdependence, and mutual obligation.

**Immigrants and undocumented community members** use this space with full standing and full protection. See Section 6.1 on safety reporting for the specific accommodation made for this community.

### 6.1 A Note on Safety Reporting for Undocumented Community Members

The general safety reporting standard in this agreement asks users to report serious safety incidents to law enforcement or appropriate community safety organizations. We recognize that for undocumented community members, contact with law enforcement may itself create danger.

For undocumented community members: you are not required to contact law enforcement to report a safety incident. Appropriate community safety organizations — domestic violence organizations, legal aid, community advocates, trusted community leaders — are legitimate and preferred reporting channels for you. Your safety is the priority. The letter of this requirement does not supersede the spirit of it.

## 7. Privacy

### 7.1 Minimum Age

Root System requires users to be at least **18 years of age**. By entering and using this platform, you affirm that you are 18 or older. If you are under 18, you may not use this platform.

This requirement is enforced at the point of entry through an explicit, non-bypassable confirmation step. It is not a checkbox buried in fine print. You are asked directly and must affirmatively confirm before accessing any community content.

We do not knowingly allow users under 18 to access the platform. If a community steward becomes aware that a user under 18 has entered, that user should be removed. Parents or guardians with concerns should contact the community steward of the relevant deployment.

### 7.2 What We Collect and Do Not Collect

**What we collect:** Only what you voluntarily post publicly. Your posts, the handle you choose, the neighborhood zone you enter, and any content you contribute to the Knowledge Archive or Coalitions.

**What we do not collect:** Email addresses, passwords, real names, precise location data, device identifiers, behavioral tracking data, or any data beyond what you choose to post publicly.

**What we do not do:** Sell data, share data with advertisers, build user profiles, or use the content or behavior of community members to train commercial systems.

**What you should know:** Posts on this platform are public within your community. Anyone in your community can read them. Do not post information you would not want your community to see. For communication outside the platform, use Signal or ProtonMail.

**A note on anonymity:** Root System does not guarantee anonymity from network-level surveillance. For high-risk organizing, use Tor Browser and consult the EFF's Surveillance Self-Defense guide at ssd.eff.org. Honest acknowledgment of our limitations protects you better than false assurances would.

### 7.3 Cryptographic Privacy Architecture

Root System uses end-to-end encryption throughout. The key things to understand:

- Your identity is an **Ed25519 cryptographic keypair** generated on your device. Your private key never leaves your device and is stored in hardware-backed secure storage. There is no password, no email, no account recovery — and no central authority that could be compelled to hand over your identity.

- Posts are encrypted with a **community key** (AES-256-GCM) before leaving your device. The relay server holds only encrypted blobs it cannot read. Even a compromised relay server reveals nothing about the content of community posts.

- **Contact information** — if you choose to share it — travels only as an encrypted blob addressed to a specific recipient's public key using X25519 key exchange. It is never stored on the relay and never transmitted in plain text.

- The relay holds encrypted blobs for up to 48 hours, then discards them. Your community's history survives on member devices, not on a server.

This architecture means that the strongest form of user privacy protection is built into the system structurally, not enforced by policy alone.

### 7.4 Time Banking and Taxes

The time bank feature allows community members to exchange time at a ratio of one hour to one hour, regardless of skill or background.

**This is not a financial transaction, and Root System is not a financial services platform.** However, time bank exchanges may have tax implications under U.S. federal law. The Internal Revenue Service may consider the fair market value of services received through a time bank to be taxable income. Root System does not provide tax advice. If you participate in the time bank regularly or at significant volume, consult a qualified tax professional regarding your obligations.

Some time banking organizations have obtained nonprofit status or IRS guidance exempting specific programs from certain tax obligations. Community stewards who operate the time bank feature at scale should seek appropriate legal and tax advice for their specific deployment.

## 8. Safety Incidents — Reporting Responsibility

**This is a community commons. The safety of the commons is a shared responsibility.**

If you witness or experience through this network: trafficking or exploitation, predatory behavior targeting vulnerable people, sexual solicitation, abuse or violence, credible threats, or any genuine safety emergency —

**Report it.**

To law enforcement (911 for immediate danger), or to:
- National Human Trafficking Hotline: 1-888-373-7888
- National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233
- RAINN: 1-800-656-4673
- Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
- Your local legal aid organization
- A trusted community advocate

See Section 6.1 for specific guidance for undocumented community members.

## 9. What This Platform Will Never Be

**Root System will never carry advertising.**
**It will never be sold.**
**It will never require payment for access.**
**It will never be acquired by a corporation.**
**It will never be used to surveil the communities that use it.**
**It will never be weaponized for political messaging.**

These are not aspirations. They are structural — the license legally prohibits commercialization, and the code contains no advertising infrastructure because none was ever built into it.

If a version of Root System ever appears that charges money, carries ads, is branded by a corporation, or suppresses the voices of the communities this platform was built to serve — it is a violation of the license and a betrayal of the commons. It is not a continuation of this project.

## 10. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

**Governing law:** This agreement and any dispute arising from use of this platform are governed by the laws of the state in which the community steward of this specific deployment operates, without regard to conflict-of-law principles. For deployments without a designated community steward, the laws of the State of Oregon govern.

For disputes that cannot be resolved at the community level, the parties agree to attempt good-faith informal resolution before pursuing legal action. This provision does not limit any party's right to seek emergency or injunctive relief.

**Severability:** If any provision of this agreement is found to be unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.

**Entire agreement:** This document, together with any community-specific standards adopted by the steward of your deployment, constitutes the entire agreement between you and the platform regarding your use of Root System.

## 12. Why This Was Built

This was built during a period of short-term disability, with time that would otherwise have been lost, for communities that have always deserved better infrastructure.

It is not a product. It is not a startup. It is not a brand.

It is a living room.

The goal was never for it to become a platform. The goal was for it to become unnecessary — for communities to be so well-connected, so well-resourced, so practiced in the art of taking care of each other, that a digital tool for it would be beside the point.

Until then: take it, use it, give it to your people, change what needs changing, and keep the spirit of it intact.

The only rule that matters: put the people at the bottom first. Always.
