Planter's Kit

You started a Root System for your community. Now what? Here's everything you need to help it take root.

📄 Printable QR flyer

When you receive your community link, we'll include a printable flyer with your QR code. Print it, cut it out, and put it up everywhere your neighbors already look.

Good places to post your flyer:

  • ✦ Library bulletin board — people already go there to find community resources
  • ✦ Post office — everyone in the neighborhood passes through
  • ✦ Food bank or pantry — people who need help most should know it's there
  • ✦ Church or faith community bulletin board — many congregations already do informal mutual aid
  • ✦ Laundromat — people sit there and read things
  • ✦ Local coffee shop or diner — ask the owner if you can leave flyers at the counter
  • ✦ School parent bulletin board — a lot of community need moves through schools
  • ✦ Hardware store or farm supply — particularly if you're in a rural area

💬 What to say when someone asks

People will ask what this is. Here's a simple way to explain it:

"It's a free community board — like a bulletin board at the library but on your phone. Neighbors post what they have to offer — a skill, something they're giving away, their time — and what they need. Then people connect directly and help each other. No ads, no account needed, no company in the middle."

If they look skeptical about "free," you can add:

"It's genuinely free. Someone built it because they wanted to help their neighborhood. There's nothing to buy."

🌱 How to seed your community board

An empty board doesn't attract people. Post a few things yourself to get it started — even if you don't need anything right now.

  • ✦ Post something you genuinely have to offer — a skill, a tool to borrow, produce from your garden, your time.
  • ✦ Post something you'd actually find helpful — even if it's small. People respond to real needs.
  • ✦ Ask a neighbor you trust to post something too. Two or three real posts is enough to show people the board is alive.
  • ✦ Don't make posts up or pretend to be multiple people. Real posts from real neighbors is the whole point.
The most common reason community boards fail is they start with nothing and people assume no one uses them. A few real posts at the start changes that completely.

📅 What to expect in the first week

  • ✦ Days 1–2: You put up flyers and post a few things yourself. The board looks new. That's fine.
  • ✦ Days 3–5: A few neighbors find it and look around. Some will post. Most will lurk. Both are fine.
  • ✦ Day 6–7: If even one exchange happens — someone gives something, someone gets something — the community has started. That's a success.
  • ✦ Don't get discouraged if it's slow. Communities build word of mouth. Every person who sees the board tells two people.

🌿 Keeping it alive

  • ✦ Renew your own posts if they're still relevant. A fresh board signals activity.
  • ✦ When you make an exchange, tell the person: "I found this through Root System. Spread the word."
  • ✦ Replace worn flyers when you see them. A flyer that's been rained on and is curling off the board is worse than no flyer.
  • ✦ The board runs itself. You don't need to manage it. Trust the community to use it well.
