About Root System

What it is, who made it, and why it's free.

What is Root System?

Root System is a free community board — like a digital bulletin board for your neighborhood. Neighbors post what they have to offer (a skill, a tool, extra produce, their time) and what they need (help with a project, something to borrow, someone to teach them something). Then they connect directly and help each other.

That's it. No marketplace. No algorithms deciding who sees what. No company sitting in the middle taking a cut.

"Think of it like the bulletin board at your library or grocery store — except it's just for your neighborhood, it's always up to date, and it works on your phone."

Who made it?

One person who wanted to help their neighbors. Not a startup. Not a nonprofit with a board and a mission statement. Just someone who thought: what if we made it easier for people to help each other?

It was built in good faith and given away freely. The communities that use it are the ones who own it.

How does it work?

Each neighborhood or community gets its own board — its own website. There's no central platform, no app to download, no account to create. You go to your community's link, read the posts, and if you see something you need or have something to offer, you respond.

Posts expire after a week so the board stays fresh. You can renew your post if it's still relevant.

If there's no Root System near you yet, you can start one. We'll set it up and send you the link — usually within a day or two.

Is it really free?

Yes. Free to use. Free to start. Free forever.

There are no premium tiers. No "free trial." No credit card required. The goal isn't to make money — it's to help communities work the way good communities have always worked: neighbors looking out for neighbors.

What about my data?

We don't collect it. We don't store it. We don't sell it. We genuinely don't want it.

Your posts are public — anyone with the link to your community can read them — so don't post your home address or anything you wouldn't put on a bulletin board at the library. But we don't track you, we don't build a profile of you, and there's no advertising following you around based on what you posted.

No account means no personal data to lose, breach, or sell.

How do I start one for my community?

Go to the Plant a Community page. Fill in your community's name, your general location, and your email address. We'll set it up and send you a link and a printable flyer you can put up at the library, the post office, the coffee shop, and wherever else your neighbors gather.

Most communities get their link within 24–48 hours.

What if someone posts something harmful?

Community members can flag posts that seem wrong. If enough people flag something, it gets hidden until it can be reviewed. There's also a community review process for appeals.

Root System isn't the right tool for every situation. For emergencies, call 911. For crisis support, call or text 988. For finding local services, call 211.
